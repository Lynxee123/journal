This week reinforced something I've been noticing more and more: good IT management is really just good information management. The computer tracking situation on Friday was a perfect example. The device existed in multiple systems with conflicting information, and the only way to actually locate it was to follow the ticket history like a breadcrumb trail. It made me appreciate how much FCN relies on documentation, not just as a formality, but as a genuine troubleshooting tool.

The compliance outreach on Thursday was another angle of the same idea. Keeping devices updated isn't just a technical task, it requires coordinating with people across multiple clinics who have their own priorities and schedules. IT here doesn't just manage hardware and software, it manages relationships with the rest of the organization. This week was a good reminder that staying on top of an IT environment at this scale means staying organized, staying communicative, and knowing which system to trust when they all start telling you something different.
