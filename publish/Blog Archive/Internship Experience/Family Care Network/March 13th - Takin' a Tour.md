Friday started with a really good conversation with George, who walked me through his path to becoming a sys admin. His advice was clear: get good at scripting and automation. We talked about silent installations, which are exactly what they sound like, deploying software in the background without any user interaction required. It's the kind of skill that scales. Instead of clicking through an installer on fifty machines, you write the script once and let it do the work.

The team meeting covered some upcoming projects including AP installs and desktop deployments at the new Admin building, which brought us to the afternoon's main event. We headed over to scope out the space and get started on mounting the wireless access points. Great plan. Very exciting. And then we opened the boxes and discovered the clamps needed to mount them to the ceiling were nowhere to be found. Just a box of access points and a dream. :(

Silver lining though: we got a full tour of the new Admin building, and it did not disappoint. Hmm, love the fresh carpet smell!

To close out the day, we headed over to NSFM for some preliminary phone placement ahead of tomorrow's big installation. 78 phones are finally going in!
