This week was one of the most eye-opening of the whole internship. Shadowing Vir on Thursday pulled back the curtain on a layer of FCN's infrastructure I hadn't really touched before, and what struck me most was the sheer amount of planning that goes into keeping EPIC running. Every potential failure point has a contingency. Production goes down? There's a read-only fallback. Internet goes down? There's BCA web. Not enough servers? Run four when you only need two or three. In a healthcare environment where providers need access to patient records at all times, redundancy isn't a luxury, it's the whole philosophy. Seeing how deliberately that's been built out gave me a new appreciation for the invisible architecture behind what looks like a simple login screen.

The data center visit and the team conversation on Friday were a good complement to all the technical depth of the week. getting to hear from Derrick, Ryan, and the rest of the team about what they actually love about their jobs was a reminder that IT is ultimately a people profession. Everyone had a different answer shaped by their role, but the common thread was genuine enthusiasm for solving problems and keeping things running for the people who depend on them. Week nine made me feel like I'm not just learning IT skills, I'm learning what it actually means to work in IT.
