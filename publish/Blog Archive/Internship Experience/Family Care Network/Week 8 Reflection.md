The D11 mystery this week was equal parts funny and instructive. Chasing down an unlabeled Ethernet port connected to something from Texas Instruments sounds like the setup to a joke, but not being able to find it is exactly the kind of problem that good cable management and port documentation is supposed to prevent. FCN manages a lot of physical locations with a lot of hardware, and when something isn't labeled or tracked, it doesn't just become inconvenient, it can become a legitimate security question. An unidentified device on the network is never something you want to shrug at, especially in healthcare.
