Nothing eases you into a Thursday quite like unboxing a fresh stack of VoIP phones. First order of business was provisioning HP Poly Edge E350s, and honestly it was smoother than expected. The team already has the whole process dialed in: unbox, connect the handset, plug into a PoE cord, and let it boot up. From there it's basically just clicking through settings like you're setting up a new iPhone. Once the phone populates its assigned extension, you slap a label on the back, package it up, and it's ready to go. We knocked out two full boxes, which felt pretty satisfying for a morning's work.

The afternoon took a little detour. I had to run over to Family Health Association to get blood drawn for a TB test, which meant missing the trip out to the Blaine Clinic. Not exactly how I planned to spend my Thursday, but you do what you gotta do. By the time I finished, the team in Blaine was still out, so I stayed back and kept shadowing help desk calls.

And the calls did not disappoint. First up was a scanner "malfunction" that turned out to be... paused. Truly a tale as old as time. Then came a credit card terminal issue at the Blaine office that the team was working on, which was a little more interesting. The fix was to remote in and run an automation script to reinstall the drivers. Just a few clicks and done! Sometimes solving IT issues requires some sophistication, and sometimes it's checking if something is turned on. Both are completely valid.
